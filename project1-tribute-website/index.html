Pakistani Islamic Scholar

Dr. Israr Ahmad was a Pakistani Islamic scholar, philosopher, and theologian. He was born in Hisar, India in 1932 and died in 2010. He was known for his work in Islamic law, philosophy, and Quranic explanation. Early life: Born into a devout Muslim family Displayed exceptional intelligence and interest in Islamic teachings Graduated from King Edward Medical University in Lahore with a Bachelor of Medicine and Surgery (MBBS) degree Education: Obtained a master's degree in Islamic Studies from the University of Karachi in 1965 Enrolled in Karachi University to study Islam in the 1960s Career: Worked for the Muslim Student's Federation in the Independence Movement Joined Jamaat-e-Islami led by Abul Ala Maududi Contributed to Islamic law, Quranic explanation, literature, and philosophy Awarded the sitara-i-imtiaz in 1982 Legacy: Followed particularly in South Asia, as well as by South Asian Muslims in the Middle East, Western Europe, and North America

Biographies

  • Name: Dr. Israr Ahmad
  • Birth: April 26, 1932, Hisar, British India
  • Migration: Moved to Pakistan in 1947
  • Education: Medical degree from King Edward Medical University
  • Career Shift: Left medicine to pursue Islamic scholarship
  • Organization: Founded Tanzeem-e-Islami in 1975
  • Key Works:
    • Bayan-ul-Quran
    • Islamic Renaissance: The Real Task Ahead
    • The Obligation of Iqamat-e-Deen
  • Philosophy: Focused on Tawheed, Ibadah, and Ummah unity
  • Death: April 14, 2010, Lahore, Pakistan
  • Legacy: Continues through books, lectures, and Tanzeem-e-Islami
